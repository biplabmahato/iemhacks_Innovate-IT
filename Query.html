Frequently Asked Questions(FAQs)
Qestion: What is crop rotation?
Answer: Crop rotation is a farming technique where different crops are planted in the same field in a specific sequence over time. This practice helps improve soil fertility, reduce pest and disease buildup, and enhance overall crop yields.
Question: What is organic farming?
Answer: Organic farming is an agricultural approach that relies on natural methods and avoids the use of synthetic pesticides, fertilizers, and genetically modified organisms. It promotes soil health, biodiversity, and sustainability.
Question: What is the role of fertilizers in agriculture?
Answer: Fertilizers are substances added to soil or plants to provide essential nutrients that support plant growth and development. These nutrients include nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, among others.
Question: How does integrated pest management (IPM) work?
Answer: It is a approach to manage pests and diseases . It combines strategies such as biological control,crop rotation,and judicious pesticide to minimize impact of pests while protecting environment,human health.
Question: What are GMOs?
Answer: Genetically Modified Organisms (GMOs) are organisms whose genetic material is altered using genetic engineering techniques. In agriculture, GMOs are often designed to have specific traits like pest resistance or increased yield.
Question:What is sustainable agriculture?
Answer: Sustainable agriculture is a farming approach that aims to meet needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet own needs. It focuses on practices conserving resources and protecting environment.
Question: How do farmers manage post-harvest handling?
Answer: Post-harvest handling involves activities to preserve and store harvested crops before they reach consumers. This includes cleaning, sorting, grading, packaging, and proper storage to maintain quality and reduce losses.
